These prompts depict the SAM-13-ROOT build target and its family as envisioned in `README.md`, `design.md`, `visual-bom-brief.md`, and `photo-shotlist.md`. The sambuca is a modern boat-shaped arched harp inspired by the Sumerian Royal Cemetery of Ur instrument — a slim curved walnut boat hull, a single curved cantilever neck rising forward, a cedar soundboard facing the player, and a row of tuning pegs along the neck. Note: the current design (2026-05-16) specifies wood-bodied zither pegs and a slip-cast ceramic neck finial; the gold-bulb-cap-with-cord-wrap tuner and BM-style flared gold cap are documented alternates only and should not be the default depiction.

## Hero Prompt

A modern boat-shaped arched harp — the sambuca — presented as a finished physical instrument in a clean product photograph. A slim, gently rounded walnut boat hull about two-thirds of a meter long, its keel curving up to a pronounced 10–15 degree skiff-like bow lift at one end, with two small carved walnut stub feet at the stern. A single laminated sapele neck sweeps up and forward from the bow in a long J-curve cantilever, topped by a small glazed slip-cast ceramic ornamental finial. A bright brass collar wraps the scarf joint where the neck meets the body. A pale Western red cedar soundboard caps the open top of the hull, facing upward; running along the soundboard-to-hull seam is a fine multi-strip lapis-blue inlay band of several thin parallel strips, and a pale rectangular mother-of-pearl-look inlay panel sits at the bow end framing a slim glued-on walnut string-holder strip. A row of slender wood-bodied zither pegs with steel hex tips runs along the upper face of the neck, with thirteen strings — wound-nylon bass and clear fluorocarbon treble — fanning from the neck pegs down to the string-holder strip. An intricate longitudinal inlay panel of Sumerian-rosette and cylinder-seal-style linework runs along the visible hull side. The instrument rests on a CNC-curve-profiled walnut cradle stand with visible Japanese-style joinery. Materials read as oiled walnut, honey cedar, warm sapele, polished brass, and deep lapis blue.
