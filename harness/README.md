# Sambuca Harness Designs

For the larger family variants (SAM-19-MID and SAM-25-CONCERT), the instrument's weight benefits from external support. Two harness approaches.

## Option A — Low-back positioning belt (SAM-19-MID)

Pattern: padded work-positioning belt (climbing / fall-arrest style) with side D-rings.

- Belt: 50–75 mm wide padded waist belt with hook-and-loop + steel buckle closure.
- D-rings: two stainless steel side D-rings (one per hip).
- Tether: short adjustable nylon strap from one or both D-rings to a fixed eyelet on the **stern** of the boat (i.e., the back end of the hull, opposite the neck).
- Sets the body's vertical load against the belt; the player still cradles the boat between their thighs but doesn't carry full weight.
- Off-the-shelf option: climbing/arborist positioning belt with the harp eyelet retrofitted.

Diagram brief: see `belt-tether.svg` (to be drawn).

## Option B — Saxophone-style shoulder harness (SAM-25-CONCERT)

Pattern: padded yoke harness, like a tenor/baritone saxophone harness (BG, Neotech, Cebulla styles).

- Yoke: padded shoulder pads with sternum cross-strap, distributing weight across both shoulders.
- Hook: heavy-duty snap-hook clipping to a fixed eyelet on the stern of the boat.
- Length: adjustable so the boat hangs at sternum height when standing.
- Standing playing position tilts the neck slightly forward; both hands access strings comfortably with the boat suspended.
- Off-the-shelf option: BG S40SH saxophone harness with the harp eyelet retrofitted; or custom-stitched leather + ballistic nylon equivalent.

Diagram brief: see `sax-harness.svg` (to be drawn).

## Mounting eyelet — common to both options

A single fixed point on the **stern** of the boat hull serves both harness options. Spec:

- Hardware: stainless steel forged eyebolt, M6, 40 mm shank.
- Mount: through-hull with internal backing plate (10 mm hardwood block glued + screwed inside the hull).
- Location: stern centerline, ~25 mm above the keel.
- Load: rated for 3× instrument weight as safety factor.
